SPQ-BR

The Schizotypal Personality Questionnaire (SPQ)
The SPQ was originally developed as a self-report measure of schizotypal personality characteristics based on the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual – III (DSM-III) definition of Schizotypal Personality Disorder (SPD). The Original SPQ included 74 items and showed good psychometric properties. Of note, 55% of subjects scoring in the top 10% of scorers met criteria for SPD based on a structured clinical interview. Convergent validity was also demonstrated in follow-up studies.

The SPQ measured a diverse set of schizotypal traits. Factor analysis of the SPQ helped define positive (i.e., “Cognitive-Perceptual”), negative (i.e., “Interpersonal”) and disorganized (i.e., “Disorganized”) subscales. Internal consistency analysis supported the use of 9 separate subscales (i.e., Ideas of Reference, Odd or Eccentric Behavior, Constricted Affect, Odd Speech, Excessive Social Anxiety, No Close Friends, Suspiciousness, Unusual Perceptual Experiences, Odd beliefs/Magical Thinking).

The Schizotypal Personality Questionnaire – Brief (SPQ-B)
A screening measure of schizotypal personality traits, based on the original SPQ was published soon after. This 22-item self-report scale was developed based on identifying individual items that showed high correlations with empirically-derived factor scores. This approach maximizes internal consistency. Initial psychometric evidence supported the SPQ-B for capturing positive (i.e., “Cognitive-Perceptual”), negative (i.e., “Interpersonal”) and disorganized (i.e., “Disorganized”) subscales. However, subsequent studies raised questions about the consistency of these factors (Compton, 2007; Axelrod et al., 2001; Mata et al., 2005; Cohen et al., 2010). The SPQ-B was not intended to capture the 9 subscales from the SPQ.

The Schizotypal Personality Questionnaire – Brief Revised (SPQ-BR)
A revised screening measure of schizotypal personality traits, based on the original SPQ, was published in 2010. The SPQ-BR features an expanded scoring system; implemented to increase sensitivity. This 32 item self-report scale was developed using Exploratory and Confirmatory Factor Analysis procedures to identify critical items that maximize both internal consistency and factor independence. Psychometric support for the SPQ-BR is quite promising.

Why use the SPQ-BR to measure schizotypal personality traits?

  1. The SPQ-BR is brief (32 items)
  2. The SPQ-BR measures positive (i.e., “Cognitive-Perceptual”), negative (i.e., “Interpersonal”) and disorganized (i.e., “Disorganized”) subscales. Internal consistency and CFA analysis supports the use of 7 separate subscales (i.e., Ideas of Reference/Suspiciousness, Odd or Eccentric Behavior, No Close Friends/Constricted Affect, Odd Speech, Excessive Social Anxiety, Unusual Perceptual Experiences, Odd beliefs/Magical Thinking.
